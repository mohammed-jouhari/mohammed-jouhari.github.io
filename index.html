About

Background & Interests

I am an Assistant Professor in Computer Science at the Faculty of Sciences, Ibn Tofail University in Kenitra, Morocco. My research interests lie at the intersection of cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, and Internet of Things (IoT).

I obtained my PhD from Ibn Tofail University in 2019, focusing on underwater wireless sensor networks — covering MAC layer optimization, topology control, and geographic routing protocols. Since then, I have held postdoctoral positions at Qatar University and Mohammed VI Polytechnic University (UM6P), working on AI-driven surveillance systems and energy-efficient IoT networks.

My current work focuses on intrusion detection systems for IoT environments using deep learning approaches, federated learning for industrial cybersecurity, and scalable LoRaWAN architectures. I actively contribute to the research community through editorial service, peer reviewing (55+ reviews for IEEE journals), and TPC membership at major IEEE conferences.
